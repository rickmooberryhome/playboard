The real problem

Recruiting gets confusing fast.

A lot of athletes want to play college football. But wanting it is not the same as knowing what to do. Families are left trying to figure out film, Hudl, X, coach emails, school fit, camps, grades, follow-ups, and timing by themselves.

The athlete waits.

He hopes coaches find him, but he is not sure who to contact, what to say, or how to follow up.

The parent worries.

You know time matters, but you do not want to become the person running the entire process.

The plan gets messy.

Emails go out randomly, follow-ups get missed, and the school list is based more on hope than fit.

How PlayBoard works

The first month builds the plan. Every month after that works the plan.

PlayBoard starts with a 360 Athlete Review to create a clear baseline. Then it becomes a weekly recruiting mentor and accountability system that helps the athlete keep moving.

PlayBoard rhythm
1.Learn where the athlete stands right now.
2.Build the recruiting board and first action plan.
3.Guide the athlete through weekly recruiting goals.
4.Keep parents updated without making them take over.

What PlayBoard gives families

A recruiting mentor, roadmap, and accountability system.

PlayBoard is designed for families who know recruiting matters but do not know how to turn that into a weekly plan.

Month 1

360 Athlete Review

We look at the athlete's profile, position, size, measurables, grades, film, Hudl, X account, current outreach, target schools, camps, visits, and recruiting gaps.

Film + presence

Highlight film, Hudl, and X

The athlete needs a recruiting presence that helps coaches quickly understand who he is and why he is worth a closer look.

School fit

Know which levels make sense

We help families think through school fit based on film, size, position, grades, timing, and current recruiting progress.

Outreach

Coach email + follow-up plan

The athlete needs to know who to contact, what to send, when to follow up, and how to track responses.

Weekly

Ongoing recruiting guidance

Each week, the athlete gets focused recruiting tasks and guidance when questions come up during the process.

Parents

Weekly parent updates

Parents see what happened, what was learned, what needs attention, and how to support without taking over.

Parent role

Support the process. Do not become the process.

Parents help organize, encourage, ask good questions, review messages, and provide support. But the athlete still needs to communicate, follow up, and take ownership.

Why this matters

College coaches want to see ownership.

PlayBoard helps the athlete build the skills to manage his own recruiting journey while still keeping the family informed. That means clearer next steps for the athlete and less guessing for the parent.

For athletes

Know what to do this week.

Recruiting can feel overwhelming when everything is one giant checklist. PlayBoard breaks the process down into the next few actions that matter most.

Example weekly tasks

Review highlight film clips
Update X recruiting profile
Email 5 realistic-fit coaches
Follow up with 3 schools
Add camps and visits to the board
